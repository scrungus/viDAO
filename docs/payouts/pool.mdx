---
title: "The StreamDAO Subscription Pool: How Funds Flow to Creators"
description: "Where your subscription fees go, how the USDC pool is held on-chain, and how funds are distributed to creators each week via the PayoutPool contract."
---

Every subscription payment on StreamDAO feeds into a single shared pool — a smart contract on the Arbitrum network that holds USDC until the weekly payout runs. The pool is the financial heart of the platform: transparent, on-chain, and governed by simple rules rather than editorial decisions.

## What the pool is

The subscription pool is a smart contract called `PayoutPool`, written in Rust and deployed on Arbitrum. It holds one thing: USDC collected from subscriber payments during the current billing period. The contract has two core functions — `deposit()` to receive funds, and `distribute()` to send them to creators — and that's intentionally all it does.

The pool balance is publicly visible on-chain at all times. You don't have to trust StreamDAO's dashboard figures; you can query the contract directly.

<Info>
  The pool currently runs on Arbitrum Sepolia (testnet) during the MVP phase. The contract address for Arbitrum One mainnet will be published here at launch.
</Info>

## How funds enter the pool

When you complete a subscription payment, your card charge is converted to USDC in the background and the platform calls `deposit()` on the PayoutPool contract. The USDC transfers from the platform's payment wallet into the contract, and the pool balance increases by that amount.

The deposit happens automatically on every successful subscription payment — including renewals. You never interact with the contract directly.

<Note>
  Approximately 5% of each subscription is retained as a protocol fee before deposit. The remainder — roughly 95% — goes into the pool for creator payouts.
</Note>

## How funds leave the pool

Once a week, StreamDAO's payout system calculates each creator's proportional share of verified watch hours and calls `distribute()` on the contract. The contract:

1. Verifies the caller is authorised (the payout system wallet)
2. Confirms the total payout doesn't exceed the current pool balance
3. Transfers USDC directly to each creator's wallet address
4. Updates the pool balance to reflect what was distributed

Every `distribute()` call produces an on-chain transaction with a hash that anyone can look up on the Arbitrum block explorer.

## The monthly reset

The pool aligns with the monthly subscription billing cycle. At the end of each billing period, the pool resets — the next month's subscriptions start building a fresh pool from zero.

This design has a deliberate consequence: **unused pool balance does not roll over**. All funds collected in a given month are distributed to creators in that month's weekly payouts. The maximum amount at risk in the contract at any point is approximately one month of total subscription revenue.

<Warning>
  If you're a creator and you miss a payout period due to account issues or a disconnected wallet, contact support promptly. Unclaimed funds from a period are distributed with the rest and cannot be retroactively recovered after the pool resets.
</Warning>

## Transparency and verification

Because the pool lives on-chain, several things are independently verifiable right now:

- **Pool balance** — query `pool_balance()` on the contract at any time
- **Deposit history** — every subscription payment produces an on-chain deposit transaction
- **Distribution history** — every weekly payout produces an on-chain distribution transaction with individual creator transfer amounts

Creator payout records in the StreamDAO dashboard include the transaction hash for each weekly distribution, so you can verify your payment on-chain without relying solely on the dashboard.

## What's coming after launch

<Info>
  **Post-MVP:** StreamDAO plans to commit a merkle root of all creator engagement scores on-chain alongside each weekly distribution. This will allow creators to independently verify that their watch hours were correctly included in the payout calculation — using a cryptographic proof, without needing to trust the platform's server-side numbers. The engagement scoring logic will also be published as open source.
</Info>
