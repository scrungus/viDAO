Uploading to viDAO takes a few minutes. Once your video is processed, it appears in the platform catalogue and immediately starts accumulating watch hours from subscribers. You'll need a creator account to upload — if you haven't been onboarded yet, reach out to the team, as creator access is currently invite-only.

## Upload requirements

Before you start, make sure your file meets these requirements:

- **File size:** 500 MB maximum
- **File format:** Any standard video format (MP4, MOV, MKV, WebM, AVI, and others)
- **Title:** Required — shown to viewers in the catalogue and on the watch page
- **Description:** Optional — appears below the player on the watch page

<Warning>
  Files over 500 MB will be rejected. If your video exceeds this limit, re-export it at a lower bitrate or resolution before uploading. A 1080p video encoded at a standard streaming bitrate (4–8 Mbps) will typically stay well under 500 MB for videos up to 10–15 minutes.
</Warning>

## How to upload

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to your dashboard">
    Sign in to your creator account and navigate to your **Dashboard**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the Upload button">
    The **Upload** button is in the top-right corner of the dashboard. Click it to open the upload form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your video file">
    Click the file picker and choose your video. Any standard video format is accepted, up to 500 MB. The upload will begin as soon as you select the file.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a title and description">
    Enter a **title** — this is required and is what viewers see when browsing the catalogue. The **description** is optional but recommended; use it to give viewers context about your video and improve discoverability.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the upload">
    Click **Submit** (or the equivalent confirm button). The platform will process your video and extract a thumbnail automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for processing">
    Once submitted, your video is processed server-side. When processing completes, it appears in your video list on the dashboard and in the platform content grid. This usually takes under a minute for most uploads.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Thumbnails

You don't need to prepare a thumbnail image. viDAO automatically generates one by extracting a frame from the first second of your video. This thumbnail is used in the catalogue grid and anywhere else your video is displayed.

<Tip>
  Because the thumbnail comes from the one-second mark, make sure your video doesn't start with a black frame, a slate, or a title card you wouldn't want used as cover art. Starting with a visually representative frame ensures a clean thumbnail.
</Tip>

## After uploading

Once your video is live, it will start accumulating verified watch hours from subscribers immediately. You can track per-video watch hours from your [creator dashboard](/creators/dashboard). Earnings from those hours will appear after the next weekly payout run.

<Accordion title="What if my upload fails?">
  If the upload is rejected, you'll see an error message indicating the reason — most commonly the file is over 500 MB or isn't a recognised video format. Check that your file meets the requirements above and try again. If you continue to see errors with a valid file, contact the viDAO team.
</Accordion>
